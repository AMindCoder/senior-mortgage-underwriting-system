MORTGAGE UNDERWRITING POLICY MANUAL
Version 2.0 - Effective January 2024

=================================================================
SECTION 1: CREDIT GUIDELINES
=================================================================

1.1 Credit Score Requirements

Minimum credit score for conventional loans: 620
- Score 740+: Excellent tier - best available rates, streamlined approval
- Score 700-739: Very Good tier - favorable rates, standard review
- Score 660-699: Good tier - standard rates, full documentation required
- Score 620-659: Fair tier - higher rates, compensating factors required
- Score below 620: Does not meet conventional loan minimum requirements

For FHA loans, minimum score is 580 with 3.5% down payment.
For VA loans, no minimum score but lenders typically require 620+.

1.2 Payment History Standards

Late payments are evaluated on a rolling 12-month and 24-month basis:
- 0 late payments in 12 months: Acceptable
- 1 late payment (30-day) in 12 months: Acceptable with explanation
- 2+ late payments in 12 months: Requires compensating factors
- Any 60-day or 90-day late payment in 12 months: High risk flag

1.3 Derogatory Credit Items

Bankruptcy:
- Chapter 7: Minimum 4 years since discharge for conventional loans
- Chapter 13: Minimum 2 years since discharge, or 4 years since dismissal
- Multiple bankruptcies: Minimum 5 years since most recent discharge

Foreclosure:
- Minimum 7 years since completion for conventional loans
- Minimum 3 years for FHA loans with extenuating circumstances

Collections:
- Medical collections: May be excluded from evaluation per CFPB guidelines
- Non-medical collections over $2,000: Must be paid or on payment plan
- Non-medical collections under $2,000: Evaluated on case-by-case basis

=================================================================
SECTION 2: INCOME AND EMPLOYMENT GUIDELINES
=================================================================

2.1 Employment Stability Requirements

W-2 Employees:
- Minimum 2 years continuous employment in same field
- Gap in employment over 6 months requires written explanation
- Recent job changes acceptable if in same field with equal or higher pay

2.2 Income Verification Standards

All income must be verified with documentation:
- W-2 employees: 2 years of W-2s, 30 days of pay stubs
- Self-employed: 2 years of personal and business tax returns
- Commission income: Must be received for minimum 2 years
- Bonus income: 2-year history required, averaged

2.3 Self-Employment Income

Self-employed borrowers require additional scrutiny:
- Minimum 2 years of self-employment in same business
- Personal and business tax returns for 2 years
- Year-to-date profit and loss statement
- Business license verification
- Income averaged over 2 years; declining income is a red flag
- If business shows loss, income may not be usable

2.4 Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio

Front-end ratio (housing expense):
- Maximum 28% for conventional loans
- Maximum 31% for FHA loans
- Maximum 41% for VA loans

Back-end ratio (total debt):
- Maximum 43% for conventional loans (QM limit)
- Maximum 50% with strong compensating factors
- Maximum 57% for FHA loans with compensating factors

Compensating factors for higher DTI:
- Credit score above 720
- Significant liquid reserves (6+ months)
- Minimal payment shock
- Long employment tenure (5+ years)

=================================================================
SECTION 3: ASSET AND RESERVE REQUIREMENTS
=================================================================

3.1 Down Payment Requirements

Conventional loans:
- Minimum 3% down for first-time buyers (with PMI)
- Minimum 5% down for repeat buyers (with PMI)
- 20% down eliminates PMI requirement
- Maximum LTV: 97% for conforming loans

FHA loans: Minimum 3.5% with credit score 580+
VA loans: No down payment required
USDA loans: No down payment required

3.2 Reserve Requirements

Reserves = liquid assets available after closing:
- Primary residence: Minimum 2 months PITI reserves
- Second home: Minimum 2 months PITI reserves
- Investment property: Minimum 6 months PITI reserves

Acceptable reserve sources:
- Checking and savings accounts
- Money market accounts
- Stocks, bonds, mutual funds (at 70% of value)
- Retirement accounts (at 60% of vested value)

3.3 Source of Funds Verification

All funds must be sourced and documented:
- Bank statements for 2 months minimum
- Large deposits (>25% of monthly income) require explanation
- Gift funds: Gift letter and documentation of transfer
- Seller concessions: Maximum 3% for conventional, 6% for FHA

3.4 Large Deposit Guidelines

Any deposit exceeding 25% of the borrower's monthly qualifying income
must be sourced with documentation. Acceptable sources include:
- Payroll deposits (verified with pay stub)
- Tax refunds (verified with IRS documentation)
- Sale of assets (verified with bill of sale)
- Gift funds (verified with gift letter and bank statements)
- Insurance proceeds (verified with claim documentation)

Unacceptable sources:
- Cash deposits without documentation
- Funds from undisclosed sources
- Borrowed funds not disclosed on application

=================================================================
SECTION 4: COLLATERAL AND PROPERTY GUIDELINES
=================================================================

4.1 Appraisal Requirements

All properties require an appraisal by a licensed appraiser:
- Conventional loans: Full appraisal required
- Appraisal must be within 120 days of closing
- Second appraisal may be required if value is disputed

4.2 Loan-to-Value (LTV) Limits

Conventional purchase:
- Maximum 97% LTV for primary residence
- Maximum 90% LTV for second home
- Maximum 85% LTV for investment property

PMI requirements:
- Required when LTV exceeds 80%
- Can be canceled when LTV reaches 78% (automatic) or 80% (borrower request)

4.3 Property Condition Standards

Properties must meet minimum habitability standards:
- Functional heating, plumbing, and electrical systems
- Sound roof with minimum 3 years remaining useful life
- No structural defects or safety hazards
- No environmental hazards (lead paint, asbestos, mold)
- Adequate access from public road

Condition ratings:
- Excellent: No repairs needed, move-in ready
- Good: Minor cosmetic updates only
- Fair: Some repairs needed but habitable
- Poor: Major repairs required - may not meet minimum standards

Properties requiring repairs over $5,000 may need repair escrow or FHA 203(k) renovation loan.

4.4 Property Types

Eligible property types:
- Single-family detached homes
- Condominiums (must be in approved project)
- Townhouses
- PUDs (Planned Unit Developments)
- 2-4 unit properties (for owner-occupants)

Ineligible property types:
- Mobile homes without permanent foundation
- Commercial properties
- Vacant land
- Properties with commercial zoning only

=================================================================
SECTION 5: COMPLIANCE AND FAIR LENDING
=================================================================

5.1 Fair Lending Act Compliance

Decisions must not consider protected characteristics:
- Race, color, national origin
- Religion
- Sex, gender identity
- Marital status, familial status
- Age (provided applicant has legal capacity)
- Disability
- Receipt of public assistance

5.2 Bias Prevention

All automated decisions must be reviewed for:
- Disparate treatment: Different treatment based on protected class
- Disparate impact: Neutral policies with disproportionate effect
- Geographic discrimination (redlining)

5.3 Documentation Requirements

All decisions must include:
- Complete audit trail of analysis and reasoning
- Clear documentation of factors considered
- Explanation of any adverse action
- Evidence that protected characteristics were not factors

=================================================================
SECTION 6: DECISION FRAMEWORK
=================================================================

6.1 Approval Criteria

Application should be APPROVED when:
- All guidelines met without exception
- Risk score 0-30
- No policy violations
- No bias flags

6.2 Conditional Approval Criteria

Application should be CONDITIONALLY APPROVED when:
- Most guidelines met but additional documentation needed
- Risk score 31-60
- Minor policy exceptions with compensating factors
- Conditions can be reasonably satisfied before closing

Common conditions:
- Additional income documentation
- Letter of explanation for credit items
- Verification of employment
- Additional asset documentation
- Property repair completion

6.3 Denial Criteria

Application should be DENIED when:
- Fundamental guidelines not met
- Risk score 61-100
- Major policy violations without compensating factors
- Applicant cannot reasonably satisfy conditions

6.4 Human Review Triggers

Cases requiring mandatory human review:
- Risk score above 65
- Any bias flags detected
- Denial recommendations
- Multiple policy exceptions requested
- Loan amount exceeding $750,000
